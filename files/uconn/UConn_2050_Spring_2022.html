Minimum Technical Skills

To be successful in this course, you will need the following technical skills:
  • Install python and third party packages (like Matplotlib) on your local computer
  • Use a terminal on your local computer (Linux, MacOS, Windows)
  • Write and run python programs on your local computer

Course Description and Objectives

By the end of the semester, students should be able to:
  • Write programs in python using imports, functions, and object-oriented programming.
  • Compare data structures and algorithms based on time and space complexity and choose the correct ones for a given problem.
  • Implement abstract data types (stacks, queues, deques, mappings, priority queues) using various data structures (lists, linked lists, doubly linked lists, heaps, trees, graphs) and algorithms.
  • Use recursive algorithms to solve problems

Labs

Labs are relatively straight-forward programming exercises on Mimir intended given you an opportunity to practice and assymilate the material that you have just heard on the lecture. You will work on the lab assingments in random pairs so that you can practice collaboration and sharing of ideas. The basic bluprint of this hands-on collaboration is that one person writes the code while the other makes the suggestions. Every 15 minutes the roles change. Often the lab assignment will need to be finished after the lab session ends and you will do that at home individually. The pairs will be random and announced shortly before the lab session. Labs will account for 10% of the final grade. The worst lab score will be dropped. Labs will become available on Mondays every week and will be due the following Sunday.

Homework

Homework will consist of weekly substantive programming assignments that are more challenging and require more time than the labs. As a result, homework will account for 25% of your final grade. As with the labs, you will need to submit your work to Mimir. These assignments will be posted on Wednesdays and due in a week from the posting. The worst assignment will be dropped. NOTE:
  • No late homework or lab assignments will be accepted 1 day past the due date. You will be allowed to submit your work at most 1 day late N times in the semester with no penalty. The number N is set generously to 5, and is implemented as "5 late tokens" on Mimir.
  • Dispute on grading must be resolved within two weeks after receiving your scores.

Collaboration

All homework assignments must be completed individually. It is okay for you to discuss a problem with a classmate as long you abide by the following conditions:
  • Each student you collaborate with should be named on the written homework assignment.
  • You must first consider each problem on your own and generate ideas on how to solve the problem.
  • You may discuss problems and ideas jointly. The goal of collaboration is to understand the high level ideas of the problem. Do not go further than this.
  • You must write solutions completely on your own.
  • Do not use other resources (outside of your textbooks and collaborators) to attempt to find the problem or the solution. This includes using the internet to search for parts of the problem.
