Course Description and Objectives

This course is an introduction to discrete mathematics and its application in computer science. We will begin from basics describing sets and other elementary objects and work on both mathematical techniques and proof skills. By the end of this course, you will be able to:
  • Write clear, thorough, and precise proofs using logic. Determine whether an argument is logically sound.
  • Gain knowledge through formal reasoning and in particular, through the application of proof by contradiction.
  • Understand discrete mathematic concepts including functions and graphs. Analyze recurrence relations that arise in computer science.
  • Understand set theory and develop proofs of equality of sets and subset relationships using the element method of proof.
  • Apply counting principles to quickly understand the order of magnitude of a computing problem, or other related domain.
  • Understand and apply elementary probability concepts.

Collaboration

All homework assignments must be completed individually. It is okay for you to discuss a problem with a classmate as long you abide by the following conditions:
  • Each student you collaborate with should be named on the written homework assignment.
  • You must first consider each problem on your own and generate ideas on how to solve the problem.
  • You may discuss problems and ideas jointly. The goal of collaboration is to understand the high level ideas of the problem. Do not go further than this.
  • You must write solutions completely on your own.
  • Do not use other resources (outside of your textbooks and collaborators) to attempt to find the problem or the solution. This includes using the internet to search for parts of the problem.
